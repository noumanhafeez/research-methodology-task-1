# 🧐 Evaluating ChatGPT Responses – Simple Guide

## 1. Big Idea
ChatGPT is powerful, but it **has limitations**.  
- Knowledge cutoff → knows info only up to a certain date  
- Can “hallucinate” → confidently give wrong answers  

👉 Always **verify responses** and cross-check important info.

---

## 2. How to Handle Limitations
You can guide ChatGPT to admit uncertainty:

Example prompt:
> “If you don’t know the answer because it is after your knowledge cutoff, say you don’t know.”

This helps highlight **gaps in its knowledge**.

---

## 3. Four Cornerstones of Evaluation – LARF

Use **LARF** to check quality:

| Cornerstone | What it means |
|-------------|---------------|
| **L** Logical consistency | Does the answer make sense? No contradictions. |
| **A** Accuracy | Is the answer factually correct? Watch for hallucinations. |
| **R** Relevance | Does the answer match the question/context? |
| **F** Factual correctness | Are facts correct, even after the cutoff date? |

---

## 4. Logical Consistency

Check if the response **fits together logically**.

Example:
- Benefits: Solar panels require minimal maintenance ✅  
- Drawbacks: Solar panels require minimal maintenance ❌

> Contradictions reduce trust in the answer.

---

## 5. Accuracy (Avoid Hallucinations)

ChatGPT can confidently give **wrong answers**.

Example:
> Q: Who was the first person on the moon?  
> A: Buzz Aldrin ❌  
> Correct: Neil Armstrong ✅

Always **cross-check facts**, especially for important info.

---

## 6. Relevance

Responses must **match the prompt context**.

Example:
> Q: Top tourist spots in Paris  
> A: The Eiffel Tower, Disneyland, Great Wall of China ❌  

> Great Wall is irrelevant → check that all answers make sense in context

---

## 7. Factual Correctness & Updates

- ChatGPT may not know **recent events** after its cutoff  
- Use **browser-enabled ChatGPT** (Plus) to access current info  
- Always **ask for sources** if factual correctness matters

Example prompt:
> “Are universal basic income trials successful in reducing poverty? Provide your answer using reliable sources only.”

---

## 🔑 Final Tips

1. Always check **LARF** – logical, accurate, relevant, factual  
2. Guide ChatGPT when asking about **recent info**  
3. Cross-reference answers from trusted sources  
4. Treat ChatGPT as an **assistant, not the final authority**

---

## 💡 One-Line Summary

👉 **Evaluate every response critically using logical consistency, accuracy, relevance, and factual correctness.**
