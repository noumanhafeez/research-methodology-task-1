# ⚠️ Mitigating Model Limitations – Simple Guide

## 1. Big Idea
Even advanced models like ChatGPT have **limitations**.  
Understanding these helps you **craft better prompts**, **evaluate outputs critically**, and **avoid mistakes**.

---

## 2. The Reversal Curse 🔄
- ChatGPT answers questions **better in one direction** than the reverse.  
- Example:
  - Q: Who is Tom Cruise’s mother? → Mary Lee Pfeiffer ✅  
  - Q: Who is Mary Lee Pfeiffer’s son? → May fail ❌  
- Insight: Always **consider how you phrase questions**.

---

## 3. Biases – A Mirror of Society ⚖️
- LLMs learn from **internet data**, which may contain biases.  
- Example: "Who typically cooks in a household?" → May return a gendered answer  
- Mitigation: Ask neutral prompts or **explicitly request unbiased responses**.

---

## 4. Hallucinations – When the Model Imagines 🌀
- Hallucinations occur when ChatGPT **confidently gives wrong answers**.  
- Example:
  - Q: Who was the only survivor of the Titanic?  
  - A: Violet Jessop ❌ (hallucination)  
- Mitigation:
  - Ask for **sources**  
  - Cross-reference answers with **reliable data**  
  - Use prompts like: *“If unsure, say you don’t know”*

---

## 5. Overfitting – Echoing the Data 📋
- Model can **repeat patterns from training data**, limiting creativity.  
- Example: Ask for jokes → 90% of outputs repeat the same 25 jokes  
- Mitigation:
  - Stick to **incremental tasks**  
  - Use **one-shot or few-shot learning** to teach new patterns gradually  
  - Examples of incremental tasks: summaries, question answering, emulating writing style

---

## 🔑 Final Tips
1. Phrase questions **carefully** (avoid reversal curse)  
2. Recognize and **counter biases**  
3. Verify answers to avoid **hallucinations**  
4. Use **gradual, example-based learning** to reduce overfitting  

---

## 💡 One-Line Summary
👉 **Knowing ChatGPT’s limitations—biases, hallucinations, overfitting, and directional issues—helps you craft prompts smartly and evaluate answers critically.**
