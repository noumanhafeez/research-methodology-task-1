# Lecture 2: What is a Prompt?

## Simple Summary

This lecture explains what a **prompt** is and how to write a good prompt when using AI tools like ChatGPT.

A **prompt** is the input or instruction you give to an AI system to get a response.  
For example:

- "What is the tallest mountain in the world?"
- "Set a timer for 10 minutes."
- "Recommend the best pizza in London."

All of these are prompts because they ask for information or action.

---

# Key Concepts

## 1. What is a Prompt?

A **prompt** is a question, instruction, or statement that you give to an AI model to generate a response.

Prompts are used everywhere in daily life:
- Searching on **Google**
- Asking **Siri or Alexa**
- Asking a **friend for advice**

With AI models like ChatGPT, the quality of the prompt determines the quality of the answer.

---

# Important Characteristics of a Good Prompt

A good prompt usually has **three important qualities**.

## 1. Clarity

Clarity means the prompt should be **easy to understand and provide enough context**.

Example:

Bad prompt: Tell me about mountains

Better prompt: What is the tallest mountain on Earth?


Clear prompts reduce confusion and give more accurate answers.

---

## 2. Specificity

Specificity means giving **clear and detailed instructions** so the AI knows exactly what you want.

Example:

Bad prompt: Set a timer
Better prompt: Set a timer for 10 minutes


The more specific the prompt is, the better the output will be.

---

## 3. Open-endedness

Sometimes prompts should allow the AI to **think creatively and generate different ideas**.

Example:
What are some creative real-world examples to teach trigonometry to students?

This allows the model to provide multiple ideas instead of a single fixed answer.

---

# Example of a Good Prompt

A teacher wants help designing a lesson plan.

**Context (Clarity):**

---

# Prompt Engineering

**Prompt Engineering** is the process of writing prompts in a way that produces the best possible results from AI models.

It combines:

**Art**
- Understanding language
- Tone
- Context

**Science**
- Knowing how AI models work
- Structuring prompts effectively

---

# Common Mistakes When Writing Prompts

There are some mistakes people make when creating prompts.

## 1. Overloading
Giving too much information in the prompt can confuse the model.

## 2. Ambiguity
If the prompt is vague, the answer may be too general.

Example: 
First Prompt: Explain trigonometry.
Improved prompt: Explain trigonometry and provide real-world examples in navigation and astronomy.


This process is called **prompt refinement**.

---

# Conclusion

Prompts act as a **bridge between your curiosity and the knowledge generated by AI**.

Better prompts lead to better results.

Instead of replacing human creativity, AI tools like ChatGPT should be used as **partners to expand ideas and generate insights**.
