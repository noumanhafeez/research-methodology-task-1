# Lecture 3: Taking Prompt Engineering to the Next Level

## Overview

This lecture explores **advanced techniques** for writing prompts in ChatGPT. It focuses on personalization, adopting personas, structuring prompts, and guiding ChatGPT through complex tasks.

The goal is to make AI responses **more tailored, creative, and useful**.

---

# 1. Infusing Persona

- Adding a persona helps control the **style, tone, audience, and length** of responses.
- Example: Instead of a generic speech, you can ask:
  > "Write a speech as if Ernest Hemingway were giving it at my university."
- This guides ChatGPT to produce outputs in a specific writing style.

---

# 2. Making It Personal

- ChatGPT can **emulate your writing style**.
- Providing examples of your writing helps AI generate outputs that match **your unique voice**.
- This technique is useful for emails, articles, and other personalized content.

---

# 3. Structuring Your Prompt

- Use **Markdown, quotation marks, and delimiters** to make prompts clear.
- Structured prompts **reduce ambiguity** and help ChatGPT understand exactly what you want.

---

# 4. Training Techniques

### Zero-shot
- Ask ChatGPT a question **without examples**.
- AI relies solely on its training knowledge.

### One-shot / Few-shot
- Provide **one or a few examples** to guide the model.
- Helps ChatGPT understand the style, format, or reasoning you want.

**Example:**

If my favorite films are 'Gladiator' and 'Sex and the City', what other movies should I watch?
- ChatGPT can then give a **curated watchlist** tailored to your tastes.

---

# 5. Problem-Solving Assistance

- Use **chain-of-thought prompting** to break complex tasks into smaller steps.
- Methods:
  1. **Zero-shot:** Ask ChatGPT for a problem-solving guide without examples.
  2. **One/few-shot:** Provide examples of frameworks ChatGPT can follow.
- This turns ChatGPT into a **helpful assistant for step-by-step reasoning**.

---

# 6. Limitations of ChatGPT

- **Biases:** May present stereotypes or misinformation.
- **Hallucinations:** Can confidently give incorrect information.
- **Overfitting:** Outputs depend on the quality of training data.
- Being aware of these helps you **use ChatGPT effectively**.

---

# 7. Anticipating Complex Workflows

- You can chain multiple prompts together for **multi-step tasks**.
- Example:
  > "Draft a startup plan focused on sustainable technology."
- Combine with requests for **frameworks, resources, or strategies**.
- Produces outputs similar to a **strategic consultancy session**.

---

# 8. Key Takeaways

- Advanced prompts allow for **personalized, creative, and multi-step outputs**.
- Using personas, structure, and examples improves results.
- Chain-of-thought and multi-step prompting help tackle **complex problems**.
- Iteratively refining prompts leads to more **accurate and useful responses**.

---

# 9. Practice

- The lecture encourages hands-on exercises to:
  - Apply personas
  - Structure prompts
  - Guide ChatGPT through step-by-step workflows
- Goal: Move from asking **“What can ChatGPT do?”** to **“What can’t ChatGPT do?”**
